Femicide Awareness in Kenya

Femicide is the killing of women because of their gender. In Kenya, this crime is tragically on the rise. This page aims to raise awareness, share crucial information on prevention, and provide resources for those affected.

Femicide doesn’t just affect the victim—it destroys families, communities, and nations. Let’s take a stand together to prevent this violence and create a society where women feel safe and valued.

Key Statistics

Over 2000 women are killed in Kenya every year due to gender-based violence.

80% of femicides are committed by intimate partners or family members.

Femicide victims often experience years of abuse before the fatal incident.

Visual representation of femicide statistics in Kenya.

Preventing Femicide

It’s essential to know the signs and take steps to prevent femicide. Here’s how you can help:

  • Recognize early signs of abuse like controlling behavior, jealousy, and violence.
  • Offer support to friends or family members in abusive relationships.
  • Encourage open conversations about relationships and gender equality.
  • Report incidents of violence to the police or a helpline.
Resources & Support

If you or someone you know is experiencing abuse, help is available. Here are some resources:

  • FIDA Kenya: Provides legal support for victims of domestic violence.
  • Women’s Empowerment Network: Offers counseling and shelter for survivors of abuse.
  • Police Helpline: Dial 999 for emergency assistance in case of violence.
  • Domestic Violence Helpline: Call 1195 for confidential help.
